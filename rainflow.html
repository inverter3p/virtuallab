The Rainflow Counting Rule

Rainflow counting breaks down a complex load history into simple, countable stress cycles for fatigue analysis. It works by applying a three-point rule:

  1. Identify Cycle: Look at three consecutive peaks/valleys (A, B, C). A full cycle is counted from A to B if the range of B-C is greater than or equal to the range of A-B.
  2. Remove & Repeat: When a full cycle is counted, points A and B are removed, and the analysis continues.
  3. Residue: Any points left at the end that don't form full cycles are counted as half-cycles.

On the Chart:

  • Solid green lines represent full cycles.
  • Dashed yellow lines represent half-cycles.
